"Killers of the Flower Moon" refers to both a non-fiction book and an upcoming film adaptation. The film adaptation, directed by Martin Scorsese, is based on Grann's book and is set to be released in the future. The story not only sheds light on a dark chapter in American history but also examines the challenges faced by marginalized communities in protecting their resources and rights. Good Will Hunting

"Good Will Hunting" is a 1997 American drama film directed by Gus Van Sant and written by Matt Damon and Ben Affleck. The film stars Matt Damon as the titular character, Will Hunting, a young janitor at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) who possesses an extraordinary talent for mathematics. "Good Will Hunting" received critical acclaim for its powerful performances, particularly by Matt Damon and Robin Williams. The film won two Academy Awards: Best Supporting Actor for Robin Williams and Best Original Screenplay for Matt Damon and Ben Affleck. The heartfelt exploration of personal growth, redemption, and human connection contributed to the film's enduring popularity.

Breaking Bad

"Breaking Bad" follows the transformation of Walter White (played by Bryan Cranston), a high school chemistry teacher in Albuquerque, New Mexico, who turns to cooking and selling methamphetamine after being diagnosed with terminal lung cancer. He partners with his former student, Jesse Pinkman (played by Aaron Paul), to enter the drug trade. The show is known for its intense character development, moral ambiguity, and exploration of the consequences of one's actions. "Breaking Bad" is often considered one of the greatest television series of all time.

The Last of Us

"The Last of Us" is set in a post-apocalyptic world where a fungal infection has turned much of the human population into violent creatures known as Clickers. The story follows Joel, a hardened survivor, and Ellie, a young girl with a mysterious immunity to the infection. Joel is tasked with escorting Ellie across the United States in the hopes of finding a group known as the Fireflies, who may hold the key to developing a cure.

Normal People

"Normal People" is a television series based on the novel of the same name by Irish author Sally Rooney. "Normal People" is praised for its realistic and sensitive portrayal of human relationships, as well as the strong performances of its lead actors.
